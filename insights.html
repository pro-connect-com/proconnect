Casual Work Guide

How to Earn More as a Casual Worker

  1. Specialize: Learn cleaning chemicals or laundry techniques to charge more
  2. Certifications: Get first aid training for KES 3K-5K to increase value
  3. Networking: Join Facebook groups like "Nairobi Domestic Workers"
  4. Reliability: Build reputation for punctuality and trustworthiness
  5. Language Skills: Basic English can increase earnings by 20-30%

Top Paying Casual Jobs:

  • Private Chef (KES 25K-50K)
  • Nanny with Driving (KES 30K-60K)
  • Elderly Caregiver (KES 20K-40K)
  • Special Needs Caregiver (KES 25K-45K)
  • House Manager (KES 35K-60K)

Where to Find Better Jobs:

  • ProConnect Job Board (coming soon)
  • Facebook Groups (Nairobi Domestic Workers)
  • Church/Community Bulletin Boards
  • Word of Mouth (Ask current employers for referrals)

Interview Success Guide

Common Kenyan Interview Questions

"Tell us about yourself"

Keep it professional - education, experience, skills relevant to the job in 2 minutes max.

"Why should we hire you?"

Highlight 2-3 key strengths with examples of how they've helped in previous roles.

"What's your expected salary?"

"Based on my research, the market rate for this position in Nairobi is KES X-Y. However, I'm open to discussion based on the full compensation package."

Tips for Success

  • Arrive 15 minutes early - Kenyan time is flexible but don't test it
  • Bring 3 copies of your CV - Many Kenyan interviews are panel-based
  • Dress one level above the job's dress code
  • Prepare 2-3 questions about the company to ask at the end
  • Follow up with a thank you email within 24 hours

Body Language Tips:

  • Firm handshake (not too strong)
  • Maintain eye contact but don't stare
  • Nod occasionally to show understanding
  • Keep hands visible (no pockets)

Salary Negotiation Guide

Kenyan Salary Negotiation Strategies

  1. Research First: Use our salary tool to know market rates before negotiating
  2. Don't Go First: Let the employer name a number first when possible
  3. Consider Benefits: Housing, transport, bonuses can be 20-40% of total comp
  4. Timing Matters: Best time is after they've decided to hire you but before signing
  5. Practice Scripts: "Given my X years experience and Y skills, I was expecting Z range"

Common Kenyan Benefits to Negotiate:

  • Housing allowance (15-30% of salary)
  • Transport allowance (KES 5K-15K)
  • Airtime allowance (KES 1K-5K)
  • NHIF & NSSF contributions
  • Annual bonus (1-2 months salary)

What to Avoid:

  • Making ultimatums unless you're willing to walk away
  • Comparing to colleagues' salaries
  • Focusing only on basic salary - consider the full package
  • Accepting immediately - "Can I have a day to consider this offer?"
